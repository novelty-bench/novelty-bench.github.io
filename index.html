About the NoveltyBench

Language models have demonstrated remarkable capabilities on standard benchmarks, yet they struggle increasingly from mode collapse, the inability to generate diverse and novel outputs. Our work introduces NoveltyBench, a benchmark designed to specifically evaluate the capability of language models to produce multiple distinct and high-quality outputs, in contrast with the "mode-seeking" evaluation paradigm in standard language model benchmarking. Our evaluation data consists of carefully curated prompts, and as well as a filtered real-world user queries from WildChat. In an evaluation of 20 language models from frontier labs, we find that the current state-of-the-art model cannot provide adequate distributional diversity compared to, say, a room of humans. This in turn measurably reduces the utility of users who interact with the system hoping to get novel and diverse generations. Our findings highlight a need for research on techniques that promote creativity in language models, and for evaluating language model outputs beyond the most likely generation.
